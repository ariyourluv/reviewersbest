Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know if a luxury beauty product is worth the price?

Look for products with clinically-proven ingredients at effective concentrations, positive reviews from beauty editors and dermatologists (not just influencers), and brands with transparent information about their research and development. Also consider the cost per use—a $150 serum that lasts four months is actually more economical than a $60 product you need to replace monthly. I also recommend checking if the product addresses a specific concern you have rather than buying something just because it's trendy.

Can I mix luxury products with drugstore products in my routine?

Absolutely! I do this all the time. Some categories are worth the splurge (like vitamin C serums or retinol treatments where formulation and stability really matter), while others perform just as well at lower price points (like basic cleansers or makeup removers). Focus your investment on treatment products where advanced formulations make the biggest difference, and save money on simple, functional products where ingredient lists are straightforward.

How long should I test a premium product before deciding if it works?

Most skincare products require at least 4-6 weeks of consistent use to show results, as this aligns with your skin's natural renewal cycle. However, you should notice that products feel good on your skin and don't cause irritation within the first few uses. For makeup and haircare, you'll typically know within a few applications whether you like the performance and finish. Be patient with treatment products but trust your instincts on anything that causes discomfort or doesn't feel right for you.

Are luxury beauty products better for sensitive skin?

Not necessarily—price doesn't automatically equal better tolerance. However, many premium brands do invest heavily in clinical testing and formulation science that can result in gentler, more effective products. If you have sensitive skin, look for products specifically formulated for sensitivity, regardless of price point, and always patch test new products before incorporating them into your full routine. Some of my favorite gentle products are luxury items, but I've also found excellent options at mid-range prices.
