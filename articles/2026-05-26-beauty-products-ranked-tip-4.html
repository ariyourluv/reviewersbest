Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I replace my ranked beauty products?

The replacement timeline depends on the product category. Mascaras and liquid eyeliners should be replaced every three months regardless of quality, as they're breeding grounds for bacteria. Cream products like moisturizers and foundations typically last 12-18 months once opened. Powder products can last several years if properly stored. Serums with active ingredients like vitamin C or retinol maintain peak efficacy for about six months after opening. Always check the PAO (period after opening) symbol on your products—it looks like an open jar with a number inside indicating months of use.

Can I mix products from different rankings or brands?

Absolutely! In fact, I encourage it. While some brands promote complete systems, most products work beautifully together regardless of brand. The key is understanding ingredient compatibility—for example, don't combine multiple active ingredients (like retinol and strong acids) without proper knowledge, as this can cause irritation. My top-ranked products from various brands often complement each other perfectly because they're formulated with quality ingredients that layer well.

Do products that rank highly work for all skin types?

Top-ranked products often earn their position partly because they're well-tolerated across multiple skin types, but no single product works for everyone. When I rank products, I note which skin types they suit best. For example, a richly emollient moisturizer might rank highest for dry skin but wouldn't suit oily skin types. Pay attention to your skin's specific needs and choose from highly ranked options within your category. Most premium brands also offer variations of their top products tailored to different skin types.

Is there a specific order I should introduce top-ranked products into my routine?

Yes, strategic introduction helps you avoid overwhelming your skin and allows you to identify which products deliver results. Start with a high-quality cleanser—it's the foundation that makes everything else work better. After two weeks, introduce a top-ranked treatment product (serum or retinol) that addresses your primary concern. Wait another two weeks before adding additional active products. This methodical approach lets your skin adjust and makes it easy to identify if a specific product causes any issues. Moisturizers and SPF can typically be introduced immediately without a waiting period.
