Frequently Asked Questions

Do smart appliances require a special internet connection?

Most fancy smart appliances work perfectly fine on standard home WiFi networks. However, because these are premium devices often streaming data or video (like refrigerator cameras), a reliable internet connection with decent speeds is important. I recommend a minimum of 25 Mbps for a home with multiple smart appliances. If you have connectivity issues, consider upgrading to a mesh WiFi system to ensure strong signals throughout your home, especially in the kitchen and laundry areas.

What happens if the company stops supporting my smart appliance?

This is a legitimate concern with any connected device. Reputable manufacturers of fancy smart appliances typically provide several years of guaranteed software support. However, most high-end smart appliances continue functioning in a "dumb" mode even if smart features become unsupported—your smart oven will still bake, your smart washer will still wash. When investing in premium smart appliances, stick with established brands that have track records of long-term support. Samsung, LG, Bosch, and similar major manufacturers are safer bets than startup companies.

Are smart appliances secure from hackers?

Security is understandably a top concern. Quality smart appliances from reputable manufacturers include robust security features like encryption and regular security updates. To maximize protection, always change default passwords, keep your appliances' firmware updated, secure your home WiFi network with a strong password, and use two-factor authentication when available. The reality is that your smart refrigerator is far less interesting to hackers than your computer or phone. That said, treating smart appliances with the same security mindset you apply to other connected devices is wise.

Can I still use smart appliances manually, or am I forced to use the app?

Absolutely! Every fancy smart appliance I've encountered works perfectly well with manual controls. The smart features are additions, not replacements. You can always walk up to your oven and turn the dial, or press buttons on your washing machine. In fact, I'd avoid any appliance that required app usage for basic functions. The best smart appliances enhance traditional controls with connectivity options, giving you flexibility in how you interact with them. Some days I love controlling everything from my phone; other times, I just push buttons like always.
